The Galactic Dragon's Picnic

Authored by Leo the Legend

First Edition • Cosmic Press

Sparky was no ordinary dragon. While most dragons guarded piles of dusty gold in cold caves, Sparky preferred the warmth of a nearby nebula. He was a Galactic Dragon, and his greatest treasure wasn't shiny coins, but the secret recipes passed down through generations of celestial chefs.

Today was a special day in the Milky Way. The stars were aligned just right for the annual Great Cosmic Picnic. Sparky adjusted his tiny white chef's hat, which sat precariously between his majestic purple horns, and looked at his checklist.

2

"Moon-sandwiches? Check. Star-flower salad? Almost ready. A guest list of the bravest space-rabbits? Definitely check!"

Sparky carefully packed the glowing moon-sandwiches into his favorite cosmic basket. Each sandwich was made from two thin slices of silver-crust bread and filled with aged moon-cheese that tasted like Swiss but sparkled like diamonds.

3

The Cheese of Tranquility

He had spent hours sourcing the finest moon-cheese from the craters of Luna. Some said the moon was made of rock, but Sparky knew better. In the right light, and with a very sharp dragon-claw, you could harvest the most delicious, aged provolone from the deepest valleys.

"Perfect texture," he murmured, sampling a small crumb. "Not too crumbly, just enough cosmic zest."

4

Through Star-Flowers

With the basket secured, Sparky took a deep breath of solar wind and leaped from his asteroid home. His wings, shimmering like liquid amethyst, beat rhythmically against the vacuum of space.

As he flew, he passed through the Fields of Orion, where star-flowers grew in wild, glowing clusters. They pulsed with soft blue light and hummed a gentle lullaby that echoed through the dragon's soul.

5

Suddenly, a blue streak zipped past him. It was the Great Comet Express, running ahead of schedule. "Watch out, Sparky!" the comet trailed in a tail of ice crystals. "The space-rabbits are getting restless!"

Safety Tip: Comets always have the right of way in the asteroid belt. They have very poor brakes.

6

The Smallest Guest

Waiting on the moon was Barnaby, the smallest space-rabbit in the quadrant. Barnaby was already munching on a stardust-carrot, his long ears twitching at every cosmic vibration.

"You're just in time, Sparky!" the rabbit squeaked. "The sun is about to duck behind the Earth, and that's when the moon-sandwiches taste best!"

7

Together, the giant dragon and the tiny rabbit spread out a checked blanket made of woven aurora borealis. It was soft as a cloud and glowed with the colors of a thousand sunsets.

They shared the sandwiches and talked about the nebulae they'd visited and the black holes they'd avoided.

8

As they ate, the sky began to rain magical sprinkles—colorful glowing dots that tasted like vanilla and starlight. "This is the best part," Barnaby sighed, letting a green dot land on his nose.

Sparky chuckled, his deep dragon laugh rumbling like distant thunder. "The secret ingredient is always the company, Barnaby."

9

The End

As the heart-shaped nebula rose in the distance, Sparky and Barnaby knew this picnic would be remembered for lightyears. Prove once again that the best recipes are those shared with friends.
